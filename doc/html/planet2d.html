Atmospheres of a super-Earth

HD 20794c

This video shows a possible scenario for the atmosphere of the relatively low-mass exoplanet HD 20794c, which orbits around its central star very closely. Here a locked-in situation is assumed, where the planet is facing always with the same side to the star. Additionally it is expected that the atmosphere is mostly build of \( \mathrm{CO_2} \).

Until the end of the simulation \( t = 800 \, \mathrm{d} \) the maximum temperature gradient is not reached. In the end, a very large temperature gradient of \( \sim 600 \, \mathrm{K} \) between day and night side is observable.

Most of the values of the table are freely chosen and denoted with an \( \circ \). The ones denoted with \( \bullet \) are from the exoplanet database. The radius \( R_0 \) is calculated by a formula given in Seager et al. (2007). The luminosity \( L \) is given by Porto de Mello et al. (2006). The video shows the capability of Fosite to run hydrodynamic simulations on two angles, with a fixed scaling parameter, which is the radius in this case.
